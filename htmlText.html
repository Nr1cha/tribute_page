Nelson Mandela

Former President of South Africa

Picture of Nelson Mandela

Timeline

  • 1918 - Born Rolihlahla Mandela at Mvezo in the Transkei
  • 1925 -Attends primary school near Qunu and recieves the name 'nelson' from a local teacher
  • 1934 - Undergoes initiation; Attends Clarkebury Boarding Institute in Engcobo.
  • 1939 - Enrols at the University College of Fort Hare, in Alice and is expelled the following year.
  • 1942 - Completes BA through the University of South Africa (UNISA)
  • 1943 - Graduates with BA from Fort Hare; Enrols for an LLB at Wits University
  • 1948 - Elected national secretary of the ANCYL
  • 1960 - Sharpeville Massacre, State of Emergency is imposed and he is among thousands detained, the ANC is banned.
  • 1964 - Elected President of the ANCYL
  • 1964 - Convicted and sentenced to life in prison on Robbin Island.
  • 1982 - Sent to Pollsmoor Prison
  • 1988 - Transferred to Victor Verster Prison in a cottage recovering from tuberculosis.
  • 1990 - Released from prison, and elected ANC Deputy President.
  • 1993 - Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize with President FW de Klerk
  • 1994 - Elected by Parliament as first president of a democratic South Africa and Inaugurated, releases autobiography "Long Walk to Freedom"
  • 1995 - Establishes the Nelson Mandela Children's Fund.
  • 2003 - Establishes the Mandela Rhodes Foundation.
  • 2013 - Passes away at the age of 95 in Johannesburg.

Noteable Quotes.

  • "The greatest glory in living lies not in never falling, but in rising every time we fall".

  • "If you talk to a man in a language he understands, that goes to his head. If you talk to him in his language, that goes to his heart."

  • "I detest racialism, because I regard it as a barbaric thing, whether it comes from a black man or a white man."

  • "There is nothing like returning to a place that remains nchanged to find the ways in which you yourself have altered".
